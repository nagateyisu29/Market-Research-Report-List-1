<p><p>The bicycle drivetrain market comprises several key components: cassettes, chains, chainrings, and derailleurs. Cassettes are the cluster of sprockets on the rear wheel that allow for gear shifting. Chains connect the front chainrings to the rear cassette, transmitting power from the pedals. Chainrings are the gear rings attached to the crankset, determining the gear ratio. Derailleurs are mechanisms that guide the chain between different gears, facilitating smooth transitions and optimizing performance across various terrains and riding conditions.</p></p>
