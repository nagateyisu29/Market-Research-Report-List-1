<p><p>Chalcogenide materials, composed of chalcogen elements like sulfur, selenium, and tellurium, can be categorized into three market types based on their electrical properties. Conductors facilitate the flow of electricity and are used in applications requiring high conductivity. Semiconductors possess intermediate conductivity, making them vital for electronic devices and sensors. Insulators, on the other hand, resist electrical flow, primarily used in applications requiring isolation. Each type plays a crucial role in various technological advancements and industries, shaping the chalcogenide materials market.</p></p>
