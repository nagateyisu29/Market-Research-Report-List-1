<p><p>The Veno-Arterial (VA) ECMO system is a life-support technology designed to provide temporary support for patients with severe cardiac and respiratory failure. It works by oxygenating the blood and facilitating circulation when the heart is unable to perform these functions effectively. The system draws blood from the venous system, oxygenates it via an external membrane, and then returns it to the arterial system, ensuring vital organs receive adequate blood flow and oxygen.</p><p>The market for Veno-Arterial ECMO systems is experiencing significant growth, driven by the rising prevalence of severe cardiac diseases, advancements in ECMO technology, and an increasing number of hospitals implementing these systems in critical care settings.
