<p><p>The glaucoma eye drops market comprises several types of medications, each targeting intraocular pressure. Prostaglandin analogs enhance fluid drainage, while beta blockers reduce fluid production. Alpha agonists work on both mechanisms, decreasing fluid production and increasing drainage. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (CAIs) also lower fluid production, while Rho kinase inhibitors offer a novel pathway by promoting drainage. Each class serves to manage glaucoma, aiming to preserve vision by controlling intraocular pressure effectively and safely.</p></p>
