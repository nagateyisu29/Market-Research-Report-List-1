Rear-mounted nacelles are positioned at the rear of the aircraft, improving aerodynamics and reducing noise. Pylons under wing connect engines directly to the wing structure, optimizing weight distribution. Clipped nacelles at the wing's edge enhance fuel efficiency and reduce drag.
